What Are Cryovolcanoes?

Cryovolcanoes, also known as ice volcanoes, are geological features on icy moons and dwarf planets that erupt volatiles such as water, ammonia, or methane instead of molten lava. They indicate potential subsurface oceans and active geology far from the Sun.

With the space industry booming—private missions to moons like Enceladus and Europa—this evocative name is ideal for space tech, astrobiology, education, gaming, or branding in the new space era.
